Lab 5: Creating Measures with DAX

Steps (Continued)

  1. Use your measures in visualizations:
    • Create a matrix with Year and Month on rows, and your new measures on columns.
    • Create a line chart showing Total Sales and Total Sales LY over time.
    • Create a card visual displaying the YoY Growth %.
  2. Format your measures:
    • Select each measure in the Fields pane.
    • Use the Modeling tab to set appropriate formatting (e.g., currency for sales, percentage for growth).

Exercise

  1. Create the four measures described in the steps above (Total Sales, Total Sales LY, YoY Growth %, Running Total Sales).
  2. Create an additional measure called "Average Order Value" that calculates the average sales amount per order.
  3. Create a measure that calculates the "Top 5 Products Sales" using the TOPN function.
  4. Use your measures to create the following visualizations:
    • A matrix showing Total Sales, Total Sales LY, and YoY Growth % by Year and Month.
    • A line chart comparing Total Sales and Running Total Sales over time.
    • A card visual showing the overall YoY Growth %.
    • A bar chart showing the Top 5 Products by sales.
  5. Format all your measures appropriately (currency, percentage, etc.).
  6. Add tooltips to your visualizations that display relevant measures.
  7. Create a new report page showcasing your DAX measures and explain what each measure calculates.

Additional Challenge

For those who finish early or want an extra challenge:

  • Create a "Moving Average" measure that calculates the 3-month moving average of Total Sales.
  • Implement a "Year to Date" toggle using a what-if parameter and DAX measures.
  • Create a measure that calculates the "Pareto" (80/20 rule) of your products, identifying which products contribute to 80% of your sales.
